Who can I talk to?

Why is this research being done?

This research explores how people learn abstract, relational concepts. The goal is to understand the cognitive processes by which people learn new concepts. Participation in this study is entirely your choice.

How long will the research last?

We expect that you will be in this research study for 30 minutes or less.

How many people will be studied?

We expect about 500 people will be in this research study.

What happens if I say yes, I want to be in this research?

If you agree to take part in this study, you will be asked to perform a task on your computer. You will be shown stimuli and will be asked to learn the category it pertains to. On certain trials you will receive feedback about the correct answer and on others you will not.

A maximum of 500 participants will be invited to participate in this research study. No individual will be invited to participate more than one time for this experiment.

What happens if I do not want to be in this research?

You can leave the research at any time and it will not be held against you. Payment will be issued only for completed experiments.

What happens if I say yes, but I change my mind later?

You can leave the research at any time it will not be held against you. Payment will be issued only for completed experiments.

Will being in this study help me any way?

We cannot promise any benefits to you or others from your taking part in this research. However, possible benefits include an improved understanding of methods used in cognitive psychology research.

What happens to the information collected for the research?

Efforts will be made to limit the use and disclosure of your personal information, including research study and medical records, to people who have a need to review this information. We cannot promise complete secrecy. Organizations that may inspect and copy your information include the IRB and other representatives of this organization.

We will make every effort to maintain the privacy of your data. Confidentiality will be ensured by using participant code numbers, rather than names, on all documents.

All electronic data will be catalogued and stored on secure computers in the laboratories of the faculty advisor and on secure servers in the Department of Psychology and Neuroscience at the University of Colorado Boulder. No identifying information will be obtained from the subjects in any of the experiments that would tie specific responses to a given individual. Sensitive information will not be collected as part of the proposed study

Can I be removed from the research without my OK?

If you do not follow the instructions of the study, you may be removed without your approval.
